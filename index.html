About

I earned my Ph.D. at UC Berkeley (2024) with Prof. Cynthia Gerlein-Safdi and am currently a Postdoctoral Researcher at Princeton University with Prof. Gabriele Villarini. I work at the intersection of hydrology and computer vision.

I was born and raised in Wuxi, China, on the shores of Tai Lake (major freshwater lake in the Yangtze River Delta), with the Grand Canal (UNESCO-listed ancient waterway) running through the city—both inspiring my pursuit of environmental engineering.

Research Focus

My research integrates remote sensing, modeling, and data-driven methods to translate complex environmental processes into decision-relevant insights. I focus on high-impact hazards including extreme flooding, wildfires, and hailstorms, and develop scalable algorithms and tools at global and large-regional scales.
